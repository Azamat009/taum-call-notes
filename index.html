TAUM — Call notes & Follow-ups

Privacy Policy

This page explains how the app accesses, uses, stores, and shares data when you use call notes, reminders, contacts, cloud backup, and subscription features.

Effective date: April 27, 2026 Contact: support@taum.kz

1. Information We Access and Collect

The app is designed to work offline first. Most note data is stored locally on your device.

We may access and collect:

  • Contact information - contact names, phone numbers, and contact identifiers from your device when needed to link notes to contacts.
  • Call-related information - phone state and completed call events, plus the last completed call’s phone number when available and permitted by Android.
  • Notes and reminders - notes you create, edit, and delete, reminder date and time, and note status values such as NEW, FOLLOW_UP, WAITING, and DONE.
  • Account and subscription information - a Firebase Authentication user identifier, subscription and entitlement status from RevenueCat, and purchase-related metadata needed to validate premium access.
  • Device and diagnostic information - crash logs and diagnostic data through Firebase Crashlytics, and usage analytics through Firebase Analytics if enabled.

2. How We Use Information

  • Create and manage call notes.
  • Match notes to existing contacts and create new contacts when you choose to do so.
  • Show follow-up reminders.
  • Detect completed calls and prompt you to add a note.
  • Back up and restore notes if you enable cloud backup.
  • Validate premium subscription status.
  • Improve stability, reliability, and performance.
  • Diagnose crashes and technical issues.

We do not use your personal data for advertising. We do not sell your personal data.

3. Local Storage

The app stores notes and related data locally on your device using a local database. If you do not enable backup, your note data remains on your device.

You can delete notes and contacts created by the app through the app or through Android system settings for contacts, depending on where the data was saved.

4. Cloud Backup and Sync

If you enable backup or sign in, the app may store a copy of your data in cloud services associated with your account.

  • Firebase Authentication for identity
  • Firebase Firestore for backup storage
  • RevenueCat for subscription entitlement validation

When cloud backup is enabled, your data may be transferred to and stored on servers operated by Google and RevenueCat or their infrastructure providers, subject to their respective privacy policies.

5. Permissions

We may request the following Android permissions:

  • READ_CONTACTS and WRITE_CONTACTS - used to find existing contacts, avoid duplicates, and create a new contact when a number is not saved yet.
  • READ_PHONE_STATE and READ_CALL_LOG - used only to detect completed calls and prepare the after-call follow-up workflow.
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS - used to show reminders and follow-up notifications.
  • INTERNET and ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE - used for optional cloud backup, analytics, crash reporting, and subscription validation.

We request only the permissions required for the app’s core features. You can deny permissions, but some features may not work.

6. Third-Party Services

  • Google Firebase - Authentication, Firestore, Analytics, and Crashlytics.
  • RevenueCat - subscription management, entitlement validation, and purchase status synchronization.

These providers may process data in accordance with their own privacy policies and terms.

7. Data Retention

  • Local note data remains on your device until you delete it or uninstall the app.
  • Cloud backup data is retained until you delete it, delete your account, or request removal, subject to technical and legal retention requirements.
  • Crash logs and analytics data are retained according to Firebase’s settings and policies.

8. Data Sharing

We share data only as needed to provide the app’s services:

  • with Google Firebase for authentication, backup, analytics, and crash reporting
  • with RevenueCat for subscription validation and entitlement management
  • with Google Play services for app distribution and subscription-related functionality

We do not sell your data or share it for advertising purposes.

9. Your Choices

  • Decline permissions, although some features may stop working.
  • Disable backup if you do not want cloud storage.
  • Delete notes and contacts created by the app.
  • Uninstall the app to remove local data from your device.
  • Contact us to request deletion of cloud-backed data, if applicable.

10. Children’s Privacy

The app is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 13.

11. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to help protect your data. However, no method of storage or transmission is completely secure.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will update the effective date and, where appropriate, notify users in the app.
